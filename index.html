Seattle to Charlotte

The five days
DAY 1

Seattle to Butte, MT

Drive
~600 miles on I-90 East. Leave at 3:00 AM.
Charge
Stop in Missoula, MT on the way. Charge full in Butte for the night.
Sleep
Walmart, 3901 Harrison Ave, Butte. About 1 mile from the charger.
Shower
Planet Fitness in Butte, or a truck stop on I-90.
Watch out
Mountain passes drain the battery faster. Arrive at the last charger before a climb with plenty of range.
Clock
You lose 1 hour entering Mountain time.
DAY 2

Butte to Rapid City, SD

Drive
~600 miles on I-90 East through Wyoming. Leave at 3:00 AM.
Charge
Stop in Billings, MT and Gillette, WY. Do not skip Gillette, chargers are far apart here.
Sleep
Walmart in Rapid City. Open 24 hours, overnight friendly.
Shower
Planet Fitness in Rapid City.
Watch out
This is the thinnest stretch for charging. Charge full at every stop. Strong crosswinds can cut your range, so keep extra.
DAY 3

Rapid City to St. Joseph, MO

Drive
~665 miles. I-90 East, then I-29 South at Sioux Falls. This is the longest day.
Charge
Stop in Murdo and Mitchell, SD. Charge full, it is open prairie between them.
Sleep
Walmart, 4201 N Belt Hwy, St. Joseph. Call the store first to be safe.
Shower
Planet Fitness in St. Joseph, or a truck stop on I-29.
Watch out
Longest day plus you lose an hour. Protect your early start and keep breaks short.
Clock
You lose 1 hour entering Central time.
DAY 4

St. Joseph to Lexington, KY

Drive
~645 miles. Through St. Louis on I-70, then I-64 East. Leave at 3:00 AM.
Charge
Stop in Columbia, MO and Louisville, KY.
Sleep
A 24-hour Walmart in Lexington. Confirm the store allows it when you arrive.
Shower
Planet Fitness in Lexington.
Clock
You lose 1 hour entering Eastern time, in Indiana.
DAY 5

Lexington to Charlotte, NC

Drive
~465 miles. I-75 South to Knoxville, I-40 East to Asheville, then I-77 South. Shortest day, you are almost home.
Charge
Stop in Knoxville, TN and Asheville, NC. Asheville gets you all the way to Charlotte.
Watch out
Near Charlotte, stay in the normal lanes on I-77. The Express Lanes cost a toll.
Let people follow along

Check in when you finish a day

Add a contact once. Then each day you mark done, a green button appears to text them that you made it, and where you are. It works from your phone with the plan open.

On your phone this opens the share sheet, so you can send it by text, WhatsApp, or anything else, and pick who gets it.

For true live location, use a real app

A web page cannot track you in the background, and this route has long dead zones in Montana, Wyoming, and the Dakotas where phones lose signal. Set one of these up before you leave:

  • Google Maps location sharing or Apple Find My. Share with your contact for the whole trip. They catch up automatically when signal returns.
  • For the empty stretches, only a satellite messenger truly works, like a Garmin inReach or Zoleo. Worth it so someone can reach you where there are no bars.

In the Google Maps app: tap your profile picture, then Location sharing, then share with your contact until you arrive. Paste that link in the box above so it rides along with every check-in.

Where you shower

Get a Planet Fitness Black Card

This is the easy answer. Buy one before you leave, about $74 for the whole trip, then cancel after. It works at every Planet Fitness in the country, and every city on your route has one.

  • You need your own card. You cannot walk in as a free guest on your own.
  • Bring your own towel, soap, and a lock. They do not provide them.
  • Check the app the night before to make sure that club is open when you arrive.

Or use a truck stop

Good backup in the empty stretches of Montana, Wyoming, and the Dakotas. Pilot, Love's, and TA have showers right off the highway.

  • About $12 to $15, paid in cash at the front desk. You do not need to buy fuel.
  • It is a private, locked room and the price includes a clean towel and soap.
Sleeping in the car

Use Camp Mode

Camp Mode keeps the air and power on all night and shuts off at 20% so you never wake up stranded. It uses about 10 to 15% of your battery overnight, a bit more in summer heat.

  • Park each night with at least 50% left. That covers the night plus your morning drive to the next charger.
  • Never sleep parked at a Supercharger. You get charged extra fees and block other people. Charge up, then move to the Walmart.
  • Keep a blanket or sleeping bag as backup so you are not stuck running the heat.

Where it is okay to park overnight

  • Walmart is the usual spot, but it changes by store. Call the store first.
  • Cracker Barrel, Cabela's, and truck stops are good backups. At truck stops, park in the car spots, not the big truck lanes.
  • Do not sleep at highway rest areas in South Dakota, Kentucky, Tennessee, North Carolina, or Illinois. It is not allowed there.
  • Back into a well-lit spot near the entrance. If a place feels wrong, just move.
Good to know before you go

The clock. Driving east you lose 3 hours over the trip. On Days 1, 3, and 4 you cross into a new time zone. A 3:00 AM start plus 10 hours puts you in closer to 5:00 PM those days, not 4:00. Set your phone to update the time automatically.

  • Tiredness is the real risk. Five long solo days is hard. Take a break every 2 hours, and if you get sleepy, nap. A Supercharger is a safe place to close your eyes. Arriving a day late is fine.
  • Summer heat and storms. It is July across the plains. Watch the weather for hail and tornado warnings, keep water in the car, and park in shade at night.
  • Move at every stop. Sitting 10 hours a day for a week is hard on your body. Walk and stretch each time you charge.
  • Bring your papers. License, insurance, and registration, plus a photo of each on your phone. Save the Tesla roadside number too. There is no spare tire, so pack a plug kit.
  • Pack food. A small cooler with ice saves money and keeps you feeling better than gas-station snacks.
  • You will lose cell signal in rural Montana, Wyoming, and the Dakotas. Download offline maps for the whole route before you leave.
  • Leave room for slack. Day 5 is short on purpose. Keep enough for one motel night in case you need a real bed.

Before you leave

    A few reminders. Chargers here come from Tesla's list, but let the car's own map plan charging on the day, it adjusts for weather and traffic. Call Walmart stores ahead about overnight parking, since it changes by location. Confirm the Planet Fitness price and hours in the app. And remember the 3:00 AM starts mean driving in the dark, so watch for deer, and stop if you get tired.
